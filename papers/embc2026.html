Structure-aware Adaptive Kernel MPPCA Denoising for Diffusion MRI
Ananya Singhal, GE HealthCare  ·  Dattesh Dayanand Shanbhag  ·  Sudhanya Chatterjee
EMBC 2026 · Toronto, Canada · Invention Disclosure — GE HealthCare
Poster · Coming Soon
To be presented at Engineering in Medicine and Biology Society, Toronto, Canada.
Abstract
We present a structure-aware adaptive kernel denoising method for high b-value diffusion MRI that dynamically adjusts kernel geometry per-voxel using gradient-informed structural priors. The method leverages local tissue anisotropy to preserve sharp tumor boundaries essential for surgical planning, whilst aggressively suppressing noise in low-anisotropy regions.

We develop a gradient-to-kernel mapping algorithm that clusters voxel-wise gradient magnitudes via k-means and monotonically assigns discrete odd-integer kernel sizes — small kernels for high-gradient tissue boundaries, large kernels for homogeneous regions. This generates a per-voxel kernel map that autonomously adapts MPPCA denoising to local anatomical complexity across the full brain volume. On HCP (MGH-USC) data at b-values of 5000–10000 s/mm², the method delivers 18% SNR gain with 96% tissue boundary preservation versus fixed-size MPPCA baselines (NYU-MPPCA, DIPY-MPPCA), producing cleaner DWI and more accurate colour FA maps.
