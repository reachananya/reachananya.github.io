ARMARecon: ARMA Convolutional Filter Based GNN for Neurodegenerative Dementia Classification
Venkata Sesha  ·  Ananya Singhal, Shiv Nadar Institute of Eminence  ·  Saurabh J. Shigwan  ·  Nitin Kumar
ISBI 2026 · London, UK
Poster · Coming Soon
To be presented at the International Symposium on Biomedical Imaging, London, UK.
Abstract
We present ARMARecon, a novel graph neural network integrating Autoregressive Moving Average (ARMA) spectral filtering with reconstruction-driven regularisation — the first approach to jointly address both over-smoothing and over-squashing in sparse-label neuroimaging graphs.

The framework represents entire cohorts as connected graphs where nodes encode neuroanatomical features and edges capture inter-subject similarity, enabling robust disease pattern extraction from limited training data. ARMA convolutions produce rational filters more expressive than standard polynomial filters, capturing long-range cortical dependencies without parameter explosion. Extensive ablation studies validate architectural choices including ARMA order selection, reconstruction weight tuning, and edge threshold selection.

On the ADNI dataset, ARMARecon achieves 98.3% accuracy for CN vs. MCI and 99.7% for CN vs. AD classification — more than 3% above GCN/GAT baselines.
