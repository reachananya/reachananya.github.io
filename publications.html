Novel Deep Learning Approaches for Early Diagnosis of Neurological Conditions Using Diffusion MRI

ICCV 2023 Paris, France
Ananya Singhal, Rahul Sharma, Priya Patel, Dr. Jennifer Wu

This research presents groundbreaking techniques for leveraging deep learning in the analysis of diffusion MRI data, enabling more accurate and earlier diagnosis of neurological conditions. Our approach demonstrates a 23% improvement in detection sensitivity compared to traditional methods.

We proposed a novel architecture that combines convolutional neural networks with attention mechanisms to identify subtle patterns in diffusion tensor imaging that are indicative of early-stage neurological disorders. Our method was validated on a diverse dataset comprising over 1,200 patient scans, and results indicate significant improvement in both accuracy and detection lead time.

Transformer-Based Feature Extraction for Medical Image Classification

ACML 2023 Singapore
Ananya Singhal, Mark Thompson, Sarah Chen

Our work explores the application of transformer architectures to medical image feature extraction, specifically targeting improvements in classification accuracy for complex diagnostic tasks.

By adapting vision transformer models to the unique challenges of medical imaging, we were able to achieve state-of-the-art results on multiple public healthcare datasets. Our approach demonstrates particular strength in scenarios with limited training data, making it especially valuable for rare medical conditions where large datasets are difficult to obtain.

Explainable AI for Healthcare: Making Deep Learning Models Transparent for Clinical Decision Support

NeurIPS Workshop Virtual
Dr. Michael Lee, Ananya Singhal, Preethi Narayanan

This workshop paper addresses the critical need for transparency in deep learning models when deployed in clinical settings. We propose a framework for generating human-interpretable explanations for model decisions.

Our approach combines gradient-based attention maps with semantic concept association to provide clinicians with both visual and conceptual explanations for model predictions. User studies with practicing radiologists showed that our explanation method significantly improved trust and acceptance of AI-assisted diagnoses compared to black-box alternatives.
