Abstract
This research presents a novel deep learning framework for early diagnosis of Frontotemporal dementia (FTD), a devastating neurodegenerative disorder affecting the frontal and temporal lobes of the brain. Our approach utilises sparse diffusion measures extracted from neuroimaging data combined with advanced deep learning techniques to automatically learn relevant features. The experimental results demonstrate promising potential in effectively distinguishing between healthy individuals and those with FTD, improving diagnostic accuracy and paving the way for future research in this critical area.
