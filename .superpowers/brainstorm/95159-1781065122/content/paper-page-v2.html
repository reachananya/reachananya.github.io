Abstract
This research presents a novel deep learning framework for early diagnosis of Frontotemporal dementia (FTD), a devastating neurodegenerative disorder affecting the frontal and temporal lobes of the brain. FTD leads to cognitive decline and behavioral changes, occurring in approximately 15–22 people per 100,000 worldwide. Early and accurate diagnosis is crucial for timely interventions and appropriate patient care. Our approach utilises sparse diffusion measures extracted from neuroimaging data combined with advanced deep learning techniques to automatically learn relevant features from the data. The experimental results demonstrate promising potential in effectively distinguishing between healthy individuals and those with FTD, improving diagnostic accuracy and paving the way for future research in this critical area.
